What is a Palindrome?

A palindrome is a word, phrase, number, or other sequence of characters that reads the same forward and backward (ignoring spaces, punctuation, and capitalization). Examples include "madam", "racecar", and the phrase "A man, a plan, a canal, Panama!".

Palindromes have fascinated people for centuries and can be found in many languages. They are often used in word games and puzzles, and have applications in computer science for string manipulation algorithms.

Our Palindrome Checker Pro tool allows you to easily check if any word or sentence is a palindrome, with options to ignore spaces, punctuation, and control case sensitivity for accurate results.
